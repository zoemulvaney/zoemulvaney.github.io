farming with.

farming with.

campaign.
date. may 2019

Irish farmers want to reap the benefits of genetically modified foods like other countries have done. The benefits include less pollution, less pesticides used, higher yields and added nutrients. This is a campaign aimed at 20 – 30 year olds that is supported by the Irish Farmers’ Association. The aim of the campaign is for this demographic to put pressure on the government to allow Irish farmers to farm with genetically modified food/crops.

Genetically modified food is an information heavy topic, so my goal was to break this down and make it more digestable to the general public.The content on the website is broken down into Facts, Fears, and Future.

The last page Help Us, allows the users to sign a petition which will put pressure on the government to change the laws around farming with genetically modified crops.I wanted the visual language to show GM foods in a more natural light, rather than a lab experiment, as they usually are portrayed. I coded the website myself using HTML, CSS and javascript.

The campaign included posters which would be dotted around Dublin to bring attention to the cause. I photographed the food myself.
